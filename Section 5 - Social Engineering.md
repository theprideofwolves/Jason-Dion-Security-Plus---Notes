Manipulative strategy used to manipulate people to get info.
#### Motivational Triggers
- Authority
	- pretend like a superior
- Urgency
	- Compelling sense of immediacy or time sensitivity to bypass authentication procedures
- Social proof
	- Using social media to promoto a scamming website so it sounds legit
- Scarcity
	- Psychological pressure, limited offer etc.
- Likability
	- associated with being nice and friendly - socially acceptable.
	- Using sexual attraction, pretending to be a friend or common interest.
- Fear
	- Ransomware etc.
	- Impersonation as FBI etc.
	- Using fear and authority.
#### Impersonation
- Impersonation
	- Assuming identity to gain access
	- Unauthorized access
	- Disruption of services
	- Complete system takeover
- Brand impersonation
	- Attacker pretends to represent a brand by using logo and other info about the brand.
- Typosquatting
	- URL hijacking - slightly changing the name of a website but still it looks like the legit one. - gnail.com - gmail.com
	- To combat this, organizations generally buy the misspelt domain names.
- Watering hole attacks
	- Targeted form of cyberattack where attackers compromise a specific website or service that their target is known to use.
	- Passive
#### Pretexting
Talking in such a way that the other person fills in knowledge gaps by themselves.

#### Phishing attacks
- Phishing
	- Fraud, deceptive emails with malicious links or malicious files.
- Spear pishing
	- Focused and targeted phishing
	- Personalised.
	- Customised
	- Has higher success rate
- Vishing
	- Phone based attacks.
	- Deceiving to get sensitive info.
- Smishing
	- using text messages
- Whaling
	- Targeting high profile people like CEOs etc.
- Business email compromise
	- Uses internal email accounts got using social engineering to get confidential data from others in the organization.
#### Preventing phishing attacks
- Anti phishing campaign
	- Educating individuals
	- Spotting and responding training
	- Generic greetings, spelling and grammar mistakes, spoofed email address.
- Recognizing a potential phishing attempt
	- Urgency
	- Unusual requests
	- Mismatched URL
		- Hover your mouse
	- Strange email address
		- Hover or expand the email to check domain
	- Poor grammar and spelling
	- Always report and don't click links.
	- Inform all users about the threat.
	- Review and update security posture or conduct more trainings.

#### Frauds and scams
- Fraud
	- Criminal act
	- To steal info or money
	- Part of social engineering
	- Identity fraud - ssn, email, etc.
	- Identity theft - assuming one's identity entirely
- Scam
	- Deceptive act or operation
	- Invoice scam.
		- Fake invoice for product they didn't order.
		- Uses pretext to get info of stuff they are using.
#### Influence campaigns
Shaping public opinion or behavior.
- Misinformation
	- Inaccurate information shared unintentionally
- Disinformation
	- Deliberate creation and spread of false information
Promoting media literacy and transparency is the solution.
#### Other social engineering attacks
- Diversion theft
	- Creating a distraction to steal info
	- DNS Spoofing attack
- Hoaxes
	- Malicious deception
	- Causes panic
	- Paired with phishing and impersonation
	- Almost verify any info
- Shoulder surfing
	- Looking over someone's shoulder to gather info.
- Dumpster diving
	- Searching through trash to find valuable information
- Eavesdropping
	- Secretly listening to private conversations
- Baiting
	- Planting a malware infected item. An usb or hdd/ssd.
- Piggybacking or Tailgating
	- Unauthorized person following an authorized person.
	- In tailgating, employee doesn't know that he is being followed
	- In piggybacking, the employee knows.
	- Insider threats commonly use piggybacking to access offices or data centers without logging their badge in the security system.
