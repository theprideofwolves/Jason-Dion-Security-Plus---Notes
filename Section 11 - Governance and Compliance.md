#### Governance
Backbone that shapes and supports org's infosec framework, guidelines, policies, procedures and standards.
First component of the GRC triad.
Leadership, structures and processes that ensure an organization's IT infrastructure aligns with its business objectives.
Directly influences the creation of guidelines, policies, procedures and standards.
Detailed steps to be followed to accomplish specific tasks with org's strategic objectives in mind.
Governance framework always adapts.
Continuous monitoring and revision takes place.

#### Governance structures
Boards
- board of directors 
- group of individuals elected by shareholders to oversee the management of an organization
Committees
- subgroups of board of directors.
- each with a specific focus
Government Entities
- Establish laws that orgs must comply with.
Centralized and Decentralized Structures
- Centralized
	- Decision making is concentrated at top levels of mngmt
- Decentralized
	- decision making throughout the org
	- faster
	- inconsistent
#### Policies
Acceptable use policy(AUP)
- Doc outlining do's and don'ts for users when interacting with on org's IT systems and resources
Information security policies
- outlines of how org's protect assets from inside and outside threats
- These cover a wide range of areas
	- Data classification
	- Access control
	- Encryption
	- Physical security
**Business continuity policies focus on how an organization will continue its critical operations during and after a disruption.
**Disaster recovery policies**
focus on how a specific org recovers from IT systems and data loss after a disaster
**Incident Response Policy**
plan for handling incidents
**Software development lifecycle**
guides how software is developed within an organization
**Change management**
ensures changes are made in a controlled and coordinated manner, minimizing risk of disruptions
#### Standards
Framework for implementing security measures
Password standards
- Ensure robustness
- minimum length, mixed characters
- change periodically etc.
- hashing and salting.
Access control standards
- determine who has access to what
- types
	- Discretionary access control
		- Owner decides who can access it.
	- Mandatory access control
		- Uses labels or classifications to determine access.
	- Role based access controls
		- makes sure everyone has access to only resources that are necessary for their job functions
**Least privilege** ensures that users have only minimum levels of access required to finish their task.
**Separation of duties** prevents any user from having complete control over a process or system.

#### Procedures
Systematic sequences of actions or steps taken to achieve a specific outcome.
E.g.:
- Emergency evacuation procedure
- Data backup procedure
Important procedures
- Change management
	- Systematic approach to dealing w changes within an org
		1. Need for change is identified
		2. plan is developed
		3. change is implemented
		4. review
	- Should have steps to rollback
- Onboarding - offboarding
	- onboarding is ensuring employee is productive asap
	- offboarding is ensuring an employee leaves in the right way
	- Manages the transition
- Playbooks
	- Checklist of actions to perform to detect and respond to a specific type of incident
	- quick. efficient and consistent

#### Compliance
Adherence to laws, guidelines and other legal specifications relevant to business 
Compliance reporting
- Systematic process of collecting and presenting data to demonstrate adherence
- Internal compliance reporting
	- report of internal policies and procedures
	- internal audit team
- External compliance reporting
	- Demonstrate compliance to external entities
Compliance monitoring
- Due diligence
	- Conducting an exhaustive review of an org's operations to identify potential compliance risks
- Due care
	- Steps taken to mitigate these risks
- Attestation
	- formal declaration by a responsible party that the org's processes and controls are complaint
- Acknowledgement
	- Recognition and acceptance of compliance requirements by all relevant parties.
- Internal monitoring
	- Regularly reviewing an organization's operations to ensure compliance with internal policies
- External monitoring
	- 3rd part reviews or audits to verify compliance with external regulations or standards
**Automation in compliance**
can streamline data collection, improve accuracy and provide real-time compliance monitoring
#### Non-compliance consequences
- Fines
- Sanctions
- Reputational Damage
- Loss of license
- Contractual impacts
